This task is about assessing whether the given YouTube video contains phone scambaiting content.
Scambaiting is the act of interacting with scammers to waste their time, and specifically for this task we are interested in scambaiting performed over phone calls.
You will be asked whether a given YouTube video is mostly scambaiting conversations with real phone scammers, partially such conversations or does not contain conversations with phone scammers at all.

  • Pay attention to the title, description and thumbnail of the video.
  • Watch and listen to 4 or 5 points in the video and determine if these points are scambaiting calls or not.
  • Using an ad-blocker is recommended for this task. uBlock Origin is an effective ad blocker for YouTube videos.

What is scambaiting?

Scambaiting is the act of interacting with scammers with the intention to decieve and waste the time of the scammers. In this context the scambaiting happens over a phone call. Check out the positive example in the examples section for more reference.

Step-by-step Instructions?

  1. Pay attention to the title and the desciption of the video. This can often tell you if a video has anything to do with scamming and scambaiting or not.
  2. As you complete tasks, the channel should also begin to provide hints on whether a video is about scambaiting. Some creators such as Kitboga and Rinoa Poison dedicate their channels to scambaiting. Note: be aware that sometimes a channel may have videos that differ from their usual videos. Channel name should be a guide, not a definitive answer.
  3. Skim through the video, to 4 or 5 points, looking and listening for whether a scambaiting phone call is occuring. This, combined with the previous steps should provide a more definitive answer to the task. If 3-5 of the points you skip to contain scambaiting, then select "Most of the video". If 1-2 of the points you skip to contain scambaiting, then select "Some of the video". If none of the points you skip to contain scambaiting, then select "None of the video".

Note: Unfortunately, a limitation of Mechanical Turk is that it is difficult/impossible to display emojis in the manner we desire. We have replaced all emojis with things like :smile:, :cat:, :radiation:
